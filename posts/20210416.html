As I worked through many test prints for digital fabrication class, I ran into a problem where the print (in this case, a cube) would move during the print; the first layer(s) were fine, but somewhere after 50% through the entire object would come away from the bed (even with a brim). My first instinct here was to re-level the bed. I ran through manual calibration with receipt paper as discussed in the previous entry, but the problem persisted. Then, I cleaned the glass bed with isopropyl alcohol, halved the inital layer sepeed from 20mms-1 to 10mms-1 and tried again. A test trying to swipe the base away from the base seemed somewhat firmer, but it could still move. After some searching, I tried experimenting with the bed temperature.
I was running all prints at the Cura default of 200/60* for nozzle/bed temps respectively. A temperature tower test (LINK) confirmed that 200* is reasonable for my Solutech filament (which checks out with the recommended 190-210* suggestion on the box). Resources online suggested trying bed temperatures up to 70* for bed temps, in 5* increments. If the PLA cools too fast, it contracts and won't stick, but too high of a temperature will result in a bulged first layer (elephant's foot). Trying out 60*, 65*, and 70* as print bed temps, I settled on the following as my 'enders game' first layer print settings; that is, these settings might be 'too much' but they seem to work with some amount of definitiveness.

setting value notes
initial layer speed 10mms-1 This is half of the cura default (20mms-1)
nozzle temp 200* this is the Cura default, aligns with suggested Solutech temps
initial bed temp 70* lowering to 65* after first layer. don't notice any bulging.
support brim curious about how a brim might 'hide' other calibration errors


A couple points of reflection on this process. There's a certain difficulty in isolating variables I'm encountering because I'm unsure how different steps will play together. For example, in picking a bed temperature, a slower initial speed might make up for a lower bed temperature or conversely a higher bed temp could compensate for a quick initial speed. Some of this might be my newness to things, but there's a combinatorial overwhelming-ness to fiddling with these settings. It's also possibel that a better bed leveling might resolve some of this issues too. Moreover, with e.g. printing with a brim, I'm slightly tentative about how using a brim on structures that don't really need them might hide other problems.
