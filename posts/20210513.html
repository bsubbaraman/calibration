2021.05.13 | blair

After working through artifact-driven projects for class, I wanted to get more familiar with the GCode itself as a way to get closer to printer settings & properties. To do so, I started with Grasshopper python scripting, thinking I could generate GCode from there. I made a simple generator for Lissajous knots, using the equations $$x = cos(n_xt + \phi_x), \quad y = cos(n_yt + \phi_y), \quad z = cos(n_zt + \phi_z)$$ where \(n_x, n_y, n_z\) need to be relatively prime for the knot to not self-intersect, and any one of the phase shifts can be assumed to be 0.
